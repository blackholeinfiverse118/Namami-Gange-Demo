## Control Surface Intelligence Architecture

---

# 1. Purpose

## 1.1 Objective

The Chandragupta Payload Contract defines the intelligence delivery architecture used by Chandragupta operational control surfaces.

The contract establishes how intelligence generated within the Marine MasterDB ecosystem is transformed into human-consumable operational awareness.

The payload architecture is designed to support:

* Operational monitoring.
* Situation awareness.
* Decision support.
* Intervention visibility.
* Explainable intelligence.
* Human supervision.

The contract provides a standardized framework for presenting intelligence generated by NICAI, Namami Gange, SVACS, and future Marine MasterDB participants.

---

## 1.2 Scope

The Chandragupta Payload Contract governs:

* Live operational intelligence.
* Historical intelligence presentation.
* Recommendation presentation.
* Confidence communication.
* Intervention tracking.
* Ownership visibility.
* Status evolution tracking.
* Multilingual presentation readiness.

The contract does not define scoring algorithms, analytical engines, or persistence mechanisms.

Those responsibilities remain within the Marine MasterDB architecture and associated product systems.

---

## 1.3 Design Principles

### Principle 1 — Human First

Payloads exist to support human understanding rather than expose internal implementation details.

### Principle 2 — Explainability First

Every displayed conclusion should be traceable to observable intelligence inputs.

### Principle 3 — Operational Clarity

Displayed information should emphasize actionability and situational awareness.

### Principle 4 — Product Independence

Payloads may consume intelligence from multiple products without collapsing product ownership boundaries.

### Principle 5 — Provenance Preservation

All operational intelligence shall retain traceability to originating products and datasets.

### Principle 6 — Deterministic Representation

Equivalent intelligence conditions should produce equivalent payload representations.

### Principle 7 — Control Center Readiness

Payloads should remain suitable for operational control-center deployment and continuous monitoring environments.


---

# 2. Chandragupta Control Surface Model

## 2.1 Control Surface Philosophy

Chandragupta operates as an intelligence consumption layer rather than an intelligence generation layer.

Its primary responsibility is presenting operationally meaningful intelligence generated elsewhere within the Marine MasterDB ecosystem.

The control surface focuses on:

* Understanding current conditions.
* Understanding changes.
* Understanding risks.
* Understanding priorities.
* Understanding recommended actions.

The control surface is not intended to expose raw database structures or implementation-specific artifacts.

---

## 2.2 Intelligence Consumption Model

Chandragupta consumes intelligence produced by:

* NICAI
* Namami Gange
* SVACS
* Shared Marine MasterDB

The control surface receives:

* Signals
* Proposals
* GIS Artifacts
* Runtime Artifacts
* Knowledge Artifacts

and converts them into operationally meaningful views.

Intelligence consumption does not alter ownership.

Ownership remains attached to the originating product.

---

## 2.3 Human Decision Support Model

The purpose of Chandragupta is to assist human decision makers rather than replace them.

Payloads should therefore answer:

### Question 1

What is happening?

### Question 2

Why is it happening?

### Question 3

How confident are we?

### Question 4

What action is recommended?

### Question 5

Who owns the decision?

### Question 6

How has the situation changed?

The payload architecture is designed around these operational questions.

# 3. Payload Architecture Overview

## 3.1 Payload Family Structure

The Chandragupta Payload Contract is organized into the following payload families:

* Live State Payloads
* Operational Signals Payloads
* Intervention Visibility Payloads
* Temporal History Payloads
* Confidence Evolution Payloads
* Priority Conditions Payloads
* Recommended Actions Payloads
* Human Ownership Payloads
* Status Transition Payloads

Each payload family exists to answer a specific operational question.

Collectively these payload families create a complete operational picture.

---

## 3.2 Shared Payload Metadata

All payload families should contain common metadata.

Required metadata includes:

* payload_id
* payload_type
* generation_timestamp
* source_product
* schema_version
* confidence_level
* entity_reference
* provenance_reference

Purpose:

Provides traceability, auditability, and interoperability across payload families.

---

## 3.3 Payload Lifecycle

Operational intelligence moves through the following lifecycle.

### Stage 1

Observation

Signals and intelligence are generated by source systems.

### Stage 2

Interpretation

Source products generate assessments, evaluations, and proposals.

### Stage 3

Payload Construction

Operational payloads are assembled according to the Chandragupta Payload Contract.

### Stage 4

Control Surface Presentation

Payloads are displayed to human operators.

### Stage 5

Human Decision

Human operators evaluate intelligence and determine appropriate actions.

### Stage 6

Historical Persistence

Payload history remains available for future review, audit, and analysis.

The payload lifecycle preserves explainability while supporting operational awareness.

# 4. Live State Payload Family

## 4.1 Purpose

The Live State Payload provides an immediate operational summary of the current condition of an entity, corridor, location, region, infrastructure asset, programme area, or intelligence target.

The Live State Payload is intended to answer the question:

"What is happening right now?"

The payload should prioritize clarity, operational awareness, and rapid human comprehension.

The Live State Payload serves as the primary entry point into Chandragupta operational views.

---

## 4.2 Required Fields

Every Live State Payload shall contain the following fields.

### payload_id

Unique payload identifier.

Purpose:

Supports traceability and auditing.

---

### entity_reference

Reference to the entity being monitored.

Examples:

* River Segment
* Inland Terminal
* Barrage
* Bridge
* Candidate Location
* Programme Region

Purpose:

Identifies the operational subject.

---

### operational_status

Current operational state.

Examples:

* Operational
* Degraded
* Warning
* Restricted
* Critical
* Closed

Purpose:

Provides immediate situation awareness.

---

### confidence_level

Current confidence associated with the displayed assessment.

Examples:

* High
* Moderate
* Low

Purpose:

Communicates certainty.

---

### status_summary

Human-readable summary describing current conditions.

Purpose:

Provides a concise operational narrative.

---

### active_conditions

List of significant conditions influencing the current state.

Examples:

* Shoaling Risk
* Congestion
* Pollution Event
* Bridge Restriction
* High Traffic

Purpose:

Provides situational context.

---

### recommended_attention_level

Priority level requiring human attention.

Examples:

* Monitor
* Review
* Action Required
* Immediate Attention

Purpose:

Supports operational prioritization.

---

### last_updated_timestamp

Timestamp associated with the latest intelligence refresh.

Purpose:

Supports freshness assessment.

---

### provenance_reference

Reference to originating intelligence sources.

Purpose:

Preserves explainability.

---

## 4.3 Example Payload

```yaml
payload_type: LIVE_STATE

entity_reference: NW1_PATNA_CORRIDOR

operational_status: Operational

confidence_level: High

status_summary:
  Corridor remains operational with stable navigability conditions.

active_conditions:
  - Moderate Vessel Traffic
  - Stable Water Levels
  - No Active Restrictions

recommended_attention_level: Monitor

last_updated_timestamp:
  2026-06-09T10:30:00Z

source_product:
  NICAI

provenance_reference:
  - CWC_RIVER_GAUGES
  - IWAI_DEPTH_OBSERVATIONS
```

---

## 4.4 Operational Interpretation Rules

Live State Payloads should:

* Prioritize current conditions.
* Avoid excessive historical detail.
* Avoid implementation-specific language.
* Emphasize operational understanding.

The payload should be understandable within a few seconds of review.

---

## 4.5 Display Expectations

Control-surface implementations should display:

* Operational status prominently.
* Confidence prominently.
* Active conditions prominently.
* Recommended attention level prominently.

Detailed supporting evidence may be accessed separately through linked payload families.

The Live State Payload functions as the operational front door for Chandragupta.

## 5.4 Signal Aggregation Rules

Operational Signals may originate from:

* NICAI
* Namami Gange
* SVACS
* Shared Marine MasterDB

Multiple signals may contribute to a single Live State assessment.

Signal aggregation shall preserve:

* Source ownership.
* Provenance.
* Confidence information.

Signal aggregation shall not alter source ownership.

---

## 5.5 Signal Presentation Rules

Control-surface implementations should display:

* Signal name.
* Current status.
* Current value.
* Trend direction.
* Confidence level.

Signals should be ordered according to operational relevance.

Critical and warning signals should be displayed before informational signals.

The objective is rapid identification of meaningful operational conditions.

---

## 5.6 Explainability Requirements

Every Live State assessment should be traceable to one or more Operational Signals.

Operators should be capable of understanding:

* Which signals contributed.
* Which signals changed.
* Which signals triggered status transitions.

Operational Signals therefore form the primary evidence layer of the Chandragupta control surface.
